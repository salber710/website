I am a 2nd year CS PhD student at Stanford studying under [James Zou](https://www.james-zou.com/), where I'm supported by the [NSF GRFP](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/grfp-nsf-graduate-research-fellowship-program). My research focuses on computational immunology and AI co-scientists. In particular, I'm developing robust machine learning methods for understanding various parts of our immune system, such as which cells are pathogenic in immune-mediated diseases. Additionally, to minimize the number of missed insights when analyzing high-dimensional datasets, I am developing agentic systems capable of autonomous dataset exploration, like [CellVoyager](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.06.03.657517v1) for scRNA-seq data.

Previously, I studied computer science and physics at UC Berkeley, where I worked in the labs of [Nir Yosef](https://www.weizmann.ac.il/immunology/yosef/) and [Elior Rahmani](https://eliorrahmani.com/home.html) on developing frameworks for increasing the robustness of single-cell analyses. I'm thankful to have been supported by the [Regents' and Chancellor's Scholarship](https://financialaid.berkeley.edu/types-of-aid-at-berkeley/scholarships/regents-and-chancellors-scholarship/) as well as the [Barry Goldwater Scholarship](https://goldwaterscholarship.gov/). I also did research in a handful of other topics, including plasma physics, computer vision, and autoimmunity at [Los Alamos National Lab](https://www.lanl.gov/), the [Biophysical Immunology Lab](https://www.bpi-oxford.com/), and [Wilson Liao's lab](https://liaolab.ucsf.edu/) respectively.

Outside of research, I love playing tennis, going to the gym, and (more recently) doing ceramics!
